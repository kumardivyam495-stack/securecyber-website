// ENCRYPTION ARCHITECTURE

Military-Grade Encryption

Our security architecture is built on the principle that we should never have access to information we don't absolutely need. This isn't just privacy protection ‚Äî it's privacy by design.

AES-256
Advanced Encryption Standard
Adopted by the US federal government & approved by NSA for classified information
256-bit key length with 14 rounds of encryption
Requires 2256 possible keys to brute force ‚Äî computationally impossible
Transforms readable data into unreadable ciphertext
Industry standard for financial institutions and government agencies
RSA-4096
RSA-4096 Key Exchange
Asymmetric encryption for secure key distribution
4096-bit keys provide quantum-resistant security
Enables secure communication without pre-shared keys
Used by banks and military installations worldwide
PFS
Perfect Forward Secrecy
Each session uses unique, freshly generated encryption keys
Even if one key is compromised, past communications remain fully secure
Prevents mass decryption of historical data in case of breach
ZKA
Zero-Knowledge Architecture
SecureCyber never stores any decryption keys
Users alone control access to their data ‚Äî always
Even SecureCyber cannot access user information
Similar to technology used by Signal, ProtonMail
